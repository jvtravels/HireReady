# Campus Placement Interview — Research Document

## Overview

Campus placement interviews are designed for freshers and early-career candidates (0-2 years experience) preparing for on-campus and off-campus recruitment drives at Indian universities. These interviews test communication clarity, project knowledge, learning mindset, teamwork, and career awareness — NOT business metrics, org-wide impact, or years of professional experience.

**Target Audience**: Final-year students, recent graduates, candidates with 0-2 years experience
**Session Duration**: 10-25 minutes
**Key Competencies**: Communication clarity, project knowledge, learning mindset, teamwork, career awareness
**Important**: College projects, hackathons, and internships are VALID experience. No penalty for lacking business metrics.

## Indian Market Context

### Campus Placement Landscape in India
- **Placement season**: July-March (pre-placement offers in July-September, main season October-February)
- **Top recruiters**: TCS (largest — 40,000+ freshers/year), Infosys (30,000+), Wipro (25,000+), Cognizant, Accenture
- **Dream companies**: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Goldman Sachs, DE Shaw, Flipkart
- **Package tiers**: 
  - Service companies: ₹3.5-7 LPA
  - Product companies: ₹10-25 LPA  
  - FAANG/quant: ₹25-60 LPA (CTC including stocks)
- **College tiers**: IIT/NIT/BITS (Tier 1), top private universities (Tier 2), state universities (Tier 3)

### Common Interview Formats by Company Type

| Company | Format | Duration | Key Focus |
|---------|--------|----------|-----------|
| TCS | NQT (National Qualifier Test) + Interview | 15-20 min | Communication, adaptability, willingness to relocate |
| Infosys | InfyTQ + HR + Technical | 20-30 min | Java/Python basics, puzzle-solving, design thinking |
| Wipro | NLTH + Interview | 15-20 min | Coding aptitude, Wipro values (integrity, customer-centricity) |
| Google | Coding + Behavioral | 45 min | Problem-solving, Googleyness |
| Amazon | LP-based behavioral + coding | 45 min | Leadership Principles, ownership |
| Flipkart | DSA + System Design (basic) + HR | 30-45 min | Problem-solving, India context |

### Common Fresher Mistakes
1. **Memorized answers**: Interviewers detect rehearsed "Tell me about yourself" responses
2. **No project depth**: "I did a project on ML" without explaining what, how, or what you learned
3. **Vague career goals**: "I want to grow in the company" — not specific enough
4. **Underselling internships**: Not connecting internship work to learnings and impact
5. **Lack of questions**: Not asking thoughtful questions about the role/team/company

## Question Bank

### Core Script (5 Questions)

1. **Tell Me About Yourself**
   - "Let's start with the basics. Tell me about yourself — your academic background, key projects, and what you're looking for in your first role."
   - Tests: Concise intro, relevant highlights, career clarity

2. **College Project**
   - "Walk me through a project you're most proud of from college. What was your specific contribution, and what did you learn from the challenges you faced?"
   - Tests: Technical depth, ownership, learning mindset

3. **5-Year Vision**
   - "Where do you see yourself in 5 years? How does this role fit into your long-term career goals?"
   - Tests: Ambition, alignment with role, realistic goals

4. **Teamwork Under Pressure**
   - "Tell me about a time you worked in a team on a tight deadline — perhaps for a college project, hackathon, or internship. How did you handle disagreements or pressure?"
   - Tests: Teamwork, conflict resolution, deadline management

### Mini Question Bank (6 Variants)
- Academic background and career interest
- College project experience and learning
- 5-year career vision and role fit
- Technical problem-solving from studies/internship
- Unequal teamwork contribution handling
- Field of study choice and career preparation

## Evaluation Framework

### FRESHER-APPROPRIATE Rubric (Always Used Regardless of Difficulty)

- **90-100**: Clear communication, specific project examples with personal contribution, shows learning mindset, articulates career goals, handles follow-ups well
  - Does NOT need business metrics or P&L impact
  - College project outcomes and learning are sufficient
  
- **75-89**: Decent structure, mentions specific projects or experiences, shows self-awareness, but could be more specific about personal role or learning

- **60-74**: Basic answers with some relevant content but lacks specifics — generic descriptions of projects, vague goals, or overly rehearsed responses

- **Below 60**: Off-topic, extremely brief, or no substantive content

### What IS Evaluated
- Communication clarity and confidence
- Project knowledge (depth, not breadth)
- Learning mindset ("I learned...", "I would do differently...")
- Teamwork and collaboration examples
- Career awareness and role alignment

### What is NOT Penalized
- Lack of business metrics (₹ revenue, P&L impact)
- No org-wide influence or stakeholder management
- Limited professional experience
- Using college projects instead of work experience
- Nervousness or slight hesitation

### Micro-Feedback Detection
| Pattern | Check | Score Impact |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| Project mention | `project\|built\|developed\|created\|designed` | +15 |
| Learning language | `learned\|grew\|improved\|realized\|discovered` | +10 |
| Clarity of approach | `approach\|method\|decided\|chose\|implemented` | +10 |
| First-person | `\bI\b` | +5 |
| Very short | < 15 words | Less penalty than other types |

## Company-Specific Preparation

### TCS (NQT Pattern)
- **Format**: Online test → Technical + HR interview
- **Focus**: Communication, adaptability, willingness to relocate/work shifts
- **Key questions**: "Are you willing to work in any city?", "Can you work night shifts?"
- **Tips**: Show process orientation, mention TCS values, express willingness to learn

### Infosys (InfyTQ Pattern)
- **Format**: InfyTQ certification → Interview
- **Focus**: Java/Python fundamentals, puzzle-solving, design thinking, digital transformation
- **Key questions**: Explain your InfyTQ project, puzzle/logical reasoning, "What is digital transformation?"
- **Tips**: Show structured thinking, connect to Infosys values (C-LIFE)

### Wipro (NLTH Pattern)
- **Format**: NLTH test → Technical + HR
- **Focus**: Coding aptitude, Spirit of Being Wipro (integrity, customer-centricity)
- **Key questions**: Code walkthrough, "Why Wipro?", ethical scenario
- **Tips**: Show integrity, mention Wipro's transformation story

### Google
- **Format**: Coding interview (Leetcode medium) + Googleyness
- **Focus**: Problem-solving, intellectual humility, collaboration
- **Key questions**: "Tell me about a time you helped a teammate", design questions
- **Tips**: Think aloud, show collaboration mindset, ask good questions

### Amazon
- **Format**: Online assessment → LP-based interview
- **Focus**: Leadership Principles — Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action
- **Key questions**: Every question maps to an LP
- **Tips**: Use "I" not "we", quantify even small impacts, show ownership

## Ideal Answer Examples

### "Tell Me About Yourself" (Strong)
> "I'm a final-year Computer Science student at [University]. My core interest is backend development — I've built 3 projects using Node.js and Python, including a real-time chat application that handles 500 concurrent users. During my internship at [Company], I worked on optimizing database queries that reduced API response time by 40%. I'm looking for a role where I can work on systems at scale and continue learning from experienced engineers. [Company] excites me because of its focus on [specific thing]."

### "College Project" (Strong)
> "I built a food delivery price comparison app as my final year project. My specific contribution was the backend — I designed the scraping system using Puppeteer and built the REST API with Express.js. The biggest challenge was handling rate limiting from different food platforms — I learned to implement exponential backoff and caching. We had 200 daily active users in our college. If I were to rebuild it, I'd use a queue-based architecture instead of synchronous scraping — that's something I learned about later and wish I'd known then."
