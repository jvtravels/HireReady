# Teaching Interview — Research Document

## Overview

Teaching interviews evaluate pedagogical thinking, classroom management, student-centered approaches, technology integration, and subject knowledge. Unlike corporate interviews, teaching interviews test the candidate's philosophy of education, ability to handle diverse learners, and commitment to student growth. STAR structure is NOT used — teaching interviews follow pedagogical assessment frameworks.

**Target Audience**: School teachers (CBSE/ICSE/State Board), college lecturers, competitive teaching exam candidates (CTET, TET, KVS, NVS), international school faculty
**Session Duration**: 10-25 minutes
**Key Competencies**: Pedagogy, classroom management, student engagement, inclusivity, curriculum thinking, technology integration
**Important**: NOT evaluated using STAR/business metrics. Teaching interviews test pedagogy and student outcomes.

### India-Specific Teaching Challenges
1. **Diverse learners**: Classes of 40-60 students with vastly different abilities
2. **Medium of instruction**: English-medium vs vernacular, code-switching challenges
3. **NEP 2020 alignment**: Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN), multi-disciplinary approach, 5+3+3+4 structure
4. **Technology gap**: Rural schools with no internet vs urban schools with smart boards
5. **Assessment reform**: Moving from rote memorization to competency-based evaluation (CBSE competency-based questions)
6. **Inclusive education**: RTE Act requirements, CWSN (Children With Special Needs) integration
7. **Parent expectations**: Marks-oriented vs holistic development tension

## Question Bank

### Core Script (5 Questions)

1. **Teaching Philosophy**
   - "What is your teaching philosophy? How do you ensure every student in a diverse classroom is engaged and learning?"
   - Tests: Inclusive pedagogy, student-centered approach, practical examples

2. **Disruptive Classroom Management**
   - "Describe how you would handle a classroom situation where a group of students is consistently disruptive and affecting other students' learning."
   - Tests: Classroom management, empathy, positive discipline

3. **Technology Integration**
   - "How do you integrate technology and modern teaching methods into your lessons? Can you give a specific example of an innovative approach you've used or would use?"
   - Tests: Tech integration, innovation, pedagogical reasoning

4. **Parent Complaint Handling**
   - "A parent complains that their child is not performing well and blames your teaching methods. How do you handle this conversation?"
   - Tests: Communication, professionalism, collaborative problem-solving

### Mini Question Bank (6 Variants)
- Teaching philosophy and student engagement
- Disruptive classroom management
- Technology and modern teaching method integration
- Parent communication and performance issues
- Handling mixed-ability classrooms
- Lesson design for diverse learners

## Evaluation Framework

### Scoring Rubric (NOT STAR)

- **90-100**: Clear teaching philosophy, uses specific classroom examples, student-centered thinking with differentiated approaches, references pedagogical methods or frameworks, addresses diverse learner needs
- **75-89**: Good pedagogical awareness with some practical examples, but missing differentiation strategies or specific classroom management techniques
- **60-74**: Basic answers about teaching with generic approaches — lacks specific examples, limited awareness of inclusive education or modern pedagogy
- **Below 60**: Off-topic, no understanding of teaching methodology, purely theoretical without practical application

## Key Pedagogical Frameworks

### Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised)
1. **Remember**: Recall facts and basic concepts
2. **Understand**: Explain ideas or concepts
3. **Apply**: Use information in new situations
4. **Analyze**: Draw connections among ideas
5. **Evaluate**: Justify a stand or decision
6. **Create**: Produce new or original work

### Constructivist Learning
- **Piaget**: Stages of cognitive development — adapt teaching to developmental stage
- **Vygotsky**: Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD) — scaffold learning just beyond current ability
- **Bruner**: Discovery learning — students construct knowledge through exploration

### NEP 2020 Key Principles
- **5+3+3+4 structure**: Foundational (3-8), Preparatory (8-11), Middle (11-14), Secondary (14-18)
- **Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN)**: By Grade 3
- **Multi-disciplinary**: No rigid arts/science/commerce streams
- **Competency-based assessment**: Moving beyond rote memorization
- **Mother tongue instruction**: First 5 years in mother tongue/regional language
- **Holistic Report Cards**: 360-degree progress tracking

### Differentiated Instruction (Carol Ann Tomlinson)
- **Content**: What students learn — same concept, different complexity
- **Process**: How they learn — varied activities based on readiness
- **Product**: How they demonstrate learning — choice in assessment
- **Environment**: Where/how the classroom is organized

## Ideal Answer Characteristics

### Teaching Philosophy Answer
1. **Core belief** (1 sentence): "I believe every child can learn given the right environment and support"
2. **Framework** (1-2 sentences): Reference a pedagogical approach (constructivism, inquiry-based, etc.)
3. **Practical example** (3-4 sentences): Specific classroom strategy you use
4. **Differentiation** (1-2 sentences): How you adapt for different learners
5. **Assessment** (1 sentence): How you measure student growth

### Example Strong Answer (Classroom Management)
> "When dealing with consistently disruptive students, I follow a three-tiered approach. First, I try to understand the root cause — disruption often signals unmet needs: boredom (they're ahead), frustration (they're behind), or personal issues. I'd have a private conversation with each student — not in front of peers — to understand what's happening.
>
> Second, I restructure the classroom environment: I'd use cooperative learning groups where these students have specific roles and responsibilities. In my experience, giving a disruptive student the role of 'discussion leader' channels their energy productively. I also use proximity management — positioning myself near the group during independent work.
>
> Third, I establish a behavior contract with clear, positive expectations — not punishments. For example, 'When the group completes the activity respectfully, they earn 5 minutes of choice time.' I also communicate with parents — not to complain, but to collaborate on a consistent approach between home and school. In my last class of 45 students, this approach reduced disruption incidents from daily to once a week within a month."
