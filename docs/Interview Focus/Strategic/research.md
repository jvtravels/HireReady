# Strategic Interview — Research Document

## Overview

Strategic interviews evaluate a candidate's ability to set vision, build roadmaps, align engineering with business goals, and make high-stakes prioritization decisions. This format is common for VP/Director-level engineering roles, Chief of Staff positions, and senior product leadership at Indian unicorns and global companies.

**Target Audience**: Senior professionals (8-20+ years), VP/Director candidates
**Session Duration**: 10-25 minutes
**Key Competencies**: Vision-setting, roadmap thinking, business alignment, stakeholder influence, prioritization discipline

---

## Indian Market Context

### Where Strategic Interviews Happen
- **FAANG India** (Google, Amazon, Microsoft): Director+ rounds focus heavily on strategic vision
- **Indian Unicorns** (Flipkart, Swiggy, Meesho, Razorpay): CTO/VP Engineering roles demand India-scale strategic thinking
- **Consulting to Tech transitions**: Ex-McKinsey/BCG candidates entering tech leadership need strategic framing
- **PE/VC-backed companies**: Board-level articulation of technical strategy

### India-Specific Strategic Challenges
1. **India scale**: 1.4B population, Tier 2/3 city expansion, vernacular language support
2. **Cost optimization**: ₹ cost per transaction matters more than in Western markets
3. **Regulatory landscape**: RBI compliance (fintech), DPDP Act (data privacy), GST integration
4. **Talent market**: High attrition (15-25% in tech), remote-first vs office-first debates
5. **Build vs Buy**: India has strong open-source communities but limited enterprise SaaS adoption

## Question Bank

### Core Script (5 Questions)

1. **VP Technical Strategy**
   - "Imagine you've just joined a company as VP of Engineering. The product has strong market fit but the tech stack is aging. How would you approach building a 3-year technical strategy?"
   - Tests: Strategic vision, prioritization, stakeholder buy-in

2. **Pivot Major Initiative**
   - "Tell me about a time you had to pivot a major initiative based on changing business conditions. How did you recognize the need and communicate the change?"
   - Tests: Adaptability, communication, decisiveness

3. **Strategy Alignment**
   - "How do you ensure engineering strategy stays aligned with business goals? Walk me through your approach to cross-functional planning."
   - Tests: Cross-functional alignment, planning rigor

### Mini Question Bank (6 Variants)
- Tech stack strategy with aging stack but PMF
- Initiative pivoting and change recognition
- Business alignment and goal connection
- Budget allocation (₹5 crore, 6 months) — resource prioritization
- Saying no to stakeholder requests — prioritization discipline
- Competitive response (competitor raises funding, underpricing)

## Evaluation Framework

### Skill Dimensions
| Skill | Weight | What It Measures |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| Leadership | Highest | Strategic vision, influence, org-level thinking |
| Problem Solving | Highest | Framework usage, trade-off analysis |
| Communication | Highest | Articulating complex strategy clearly |
| Structure | High | Organized thinking, roadmap coherence |
| Business Impact | High | Revenue/growth connection |
| Specificity | Medium | Data-driven reasoning |
| Adaptability | Medium | Pivoting under uncertainty |

### Scoring Expectations
- **90-100**: Clear strategic frameworks, quantified trade-offs, stakeholder influence strategy, competitive analysis, data-driven prioritization
- **75-89**: Good strategic thinking but missing quantified business impact or stakeholder management details
- **60-74**: Generic strategic answers without frameworks, vague prioritization
- **Below 60**: No strategic thinking demonstrated, purely tactical answers

---

## Ideal Answer Characteristics

A strong strategic answer includes:
1. **Framework**: Use a structured approach (e.g., "I'd evaluate across three dimensions...")
2. **Data**: Reference metrics, market data, competitive intelligence
3. **Trade-offs**: Explicitly name what you're NOT doing and why
4. **Stakeholder map**: Who needs to be aligned, how you'd get buy-in
5. **Timeline**: Phased approach with milestones
6. **Risk mitigation**: What could go wrong and how you'd handle it

### Example Strong Answer
> "When building a 3-year technical strategy, I'd start with a 30-day diagnostic phase — auditing our current architecture, talking to 20+ engineers across teams, and mapping technical debt to business impact. I'd structure the strategy across three pillars: reliability (year 1 — reduce incident rate by 60%), velocity (year 2 — CI/CD < 30 min), and innovation (year 3 — self-serve platform). The key trade-off is between migration speed and feature velocity — I'd propose a 70/30 split initially, shifting to 50/50 by Q3. For buy-in, I'd present to the leadership team with a cost-of-inaction analysis: every month of delay costs approximately ₹X in engineering productivity."
