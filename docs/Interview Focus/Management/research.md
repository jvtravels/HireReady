# Management Interview — Research Document

## Overview

Management interviews evaluate leadership style, team building, change management, cross-functional influence, and the ability to drive results through people rather than individual contribution. This format is used for Engineering Manager, Director, VP, General Manager, and Operations Manager roles. The focus is on people management depth, organizational thinking, and measurable team-level outcomes.

**Target Audience**: Engineering Managers, Directors, VPs, Operations Managers, Program Managers
**Session Duration**: 10-25 minutes
**Key Competencies**: Leadership style, team building, change management, underperformance handling, cross-functional influence, talent retention

## Indian Market Context

### Management Role Landscape in India

| Level | Title | Typical Span | TC Range (India) |
|-------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| M1 | Engineering Manager | 5-8 ICs | ₹25-50 LPA |
| M2 | Senior EM / Associate Director | 2-3 teams (15-25) | ₹40-80 LPA |
| M3 | Director of Engineering | 4-6 teams (30-60) | ₹60-1.2 Cr |
| M4 | VP Engineering | 50-200+ engineers | ₹1-3 Cr |
| M5 | CTO / SVP | Entire engineering org | ₹2-5 Cr+ |

### India-Specific Management Challenges
1. **High attrition**: India tech attrition is 15-25% annually — retention is a top priority
2. **Remote/hybrid**: Post-COVID, managing distributed teams across India + global offices
3. **Notice periods**: 60-90 day notice periods mean hiring and retention planning is complex
4. **Fresher management**: IT services hire 30,000+ freshers/year — onboarding at scale
5. **Multi-generation teams**: Managing teams spanning Gen Z (freshers) to Gen X (senior architects)
6. **Cultural hierarchy**: Shifting from hierarchical to flat structures — difficult in traditional Indian companies
7. **Moonlighting**: Managing dual employment concerns (became major issue post-2022)

### Common Interview Formats
- **FAANG**: Bar raiser + hiring committee (Amazon LP: "Hire and Develop the Best", "Earn Trust")
- **Startups**: Founder/CTO interview (culture fit + execution ability)
- **IT Services**: Senior leadership panel (process adherence + client management)
- **Indian conglomerates**: Multi-round panel with HR + business + technical leaders

## Question Bank

### Core Script (5 Questions)

1. **Management Philosophy**
   - "Tell me about your management philosophy. How do you balance getting results with keeping your team motivated and engaged?"
   - Tests: Leadership style clarity, people-first thinking, results orientation

2. **Change Management**
   - "Describe a situation where you had to manage a team through a significant change or reorganization. What was your approach and what challenges did you face?"
   - Tests: Change management, communication, empathy

3. **Underperformance Handling**
   - "How do you handle underperformance on your team? Walk me through a specific example where you had to address a team member who wasn't meeting expectations."
   - Tests: Difficult conversations, fairness, development orientation

4. **Cross-Functional Leadership**
   - "Tell me about a cross-functional initiative you led. How did you align different departments with competing priorities toward a shared goal?"
   - Tests: Influence without authority, stakeholder management, strategic thinking

### Mini Question Bank (6 Variants)
- Team building and scaling approach
- Difficult feedback delivery to team members
- Balancing technical excellence vs delivery speed
- Retention of top performers
- Handling missed sprint commitments
- Remote/distributed team inclusion

## Evaluation Framework

### Skill Dimensions
| Skill | Weight | What It Measures |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| Leadership | Highest | Style, vision, inspiration |
| People Management | Highest | Coaching, feedback, development |
| Stakeholder Alignment | Highest | Cross-functional influence |
| Strategic Thinking | Highest | Org-level decision making |
| Communication | High | Clarity in difficult situations |
| Problem Solving | High | Organizational problem diagnosis |
| Empathy | Medium | Understanding team dynamics |

### Scoring Expectations
- **90-100**: Clear leadership philosophy with specific examples, quantified team outcomes (team size, attrition reduction, velocity improvement), demonstrated people development, cross-functional influence with measurable results
- **75-89**: Good people focus but missing quantified team impact or leadership philosophy is generic
- **60-74**: Tactical management answers without strategic thinking, limited people focus, no team metrics
- **Below 60**: No management depth, purely individual contributor thinking

### Micro-Feedback Detection
| Pattern | Check | Score Impact |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| People focus | `team\|reports\|coaching\|delegate\|feedback\|1:1\|mentoring` | +15 |
| Team scale | Numbers: team size, span of control | +10 |
| Outcomes | `result\|impact\|improved\|reduced\|delivered` | +10 |
| Process/framework | `framework\|process\|methodology\|cadence` | +5 |
| Short answer | < 25 words | -15 |

## Key Management Frameworks (for Reference)

### Situational Leadership (Hersey-Blanchard)
- **Directing**: New team members — high task, low relationship
- **Coaching**: Developing team members — high task, high relationship
- **Supporting**: Competent team members — low task, high relationship
- **Delegating**: Expert team members — low task, low relationship

### Manager's Triangle
1. **People**: Hiring, retention, development, performance management
2. **Process**: Sprint methodology, incident management, code review standards
3. **Product**: Roadmap alignment, technical strategy, quality standards

### DORA Metrics (Engineering Management)
- **Deployment frequency**: How often does your team deploy?
- **Lead time for changes**: How long from commit to production?
- **Change failure rate**: What % of deployments cause failures?
- **Time to restore service**: How quickly do you recover from incidents?

## Ideal Answer Characteristics

### Management Philosophy Answer
1. **State your style** (1 sentence): "I believe in servant leadership — my job is to remove obstacles for my team"
2. **Framework** (2 sentences): "I use a coaching approach for growing engineers and a delegating approach for senior staff"
3. **Specific example** (3-4 sentences): How this philosophy played out in a real situation
4. **Metrics** (1-2 sentences): Team size, retention rate, velocity improvement, NPS scores

### Underperformance Handling Answer
1. **Diagnosis**: "First, I identify whether it's a skill gap, motivation issue, or personal problem"
2. **Conversation**: "I have a direct, private conversation with specific examples of the performance gap"
3. **Plan**: "We co-create a 30-60-90 day improvement plan with clear, measurable milestones"
4. **Support**: "I increase our 1:1 frequency and provide resources — pairing, training, reduced scope"
5. **Outcome**: "In most cases, this works. When it doesn't, I follow a transparent exit process"
6. **Impact**: "Last year, I turned around 3 out of 4 underperformers; the 4th exited amicably"
