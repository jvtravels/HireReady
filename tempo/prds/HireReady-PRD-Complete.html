**Product Requirements Document** # HireReady AI-Powered Mock Interview Platform April 2026 · Production Build Product Requirements · Roadmap · User Personas\ Competitor Analysis · User Journeys · Brand Guide --- # HireReady — Product Requirements Document **Product:** HireReady (formerly Level Up Interviews) | **Date:** April 2026 | **Status:** Live (Production) --- ## TL;DR HireReady is a premium-positioned web platform that lets experienced professionals practice realistic, AI-driven mock interviews with instant scored feedback. It targets **B2C individual professionals** preparing for senior-level interviews. Users configure their interview type, difficulty, and target role, then engage in a real-time conversational mock interview with an AI interviewer that speaks questions aloud. After completion, they receive detailed scoring, skill breakdowns, and actionable coaching feedback. Monetized via Razorpay with weekly (₹49) and monthly (₹149) plans. --- ## Background Job interview preparation for senior professionals is underserved. Existing tools are either too generic (question banks), too informal (peer practice), or too expensive (1:1 human coaching at $200+/session). HireReady combines AI-driven question generation, real-time conversational simulation with text-to-speech, speech recognition for candidate responses, and role-specific scoring into a single platform. ### Market Context - **Human coaching:** $200–400/session, requires scheduling, not on-demand - **Generic prep tools:** Question banks (Glassdoor, LeetCode) — no personalization, no simulation - **AI competitors:** Emerging but lack luxury positioning and role-specific depth for senior professionals **Differentiation:** Luxury-grade dark-theme design, adaptive AI that tailors questions to resume and role, Google Cloud Neural2 TTS voices, and Indian market pricing via Razorpay. --- ## Target Users ### Individual Professionals (B2C) - **Who:** Senior professionals preparing for leadership, PM, engineering, or strategic roles. Recently laid off, transitioning, or upskilling. - **Pain point:** No way to practice realistic, personalized interviews on-demand without scheduling a human coach. - **Impact:** Users gain confidence, receive structured feedback on communication, structure, leadership, and problem-solving skills. --- ## Product Overview (As Built) ### Live Features #### Landing Page (`/`) - Cinematic dark-theme landing with particle canvas hero animation - Parallax scroll effects, animated typography, mouse-tracking gradient - Value proposition sections, feature grid, pricing cards, FAQ accordion - Social proof section, Unsplash background imagery - "Start Free" CTA — no credit card required - Inline Razorpay checkout for upgrading from landing page #### Authentication (`/login`, `/signup`) - Email/password signup and login via Supabase Auth - Google OAuth (Sign in with Google) - Password reset flow (`/reset-password`) - Email confirmation with spam folder guidance - Session persistence via Supabase localStorage tokens - Auto-redirect to onboarding for new users #### Onboarding (`/onboarding`) - Multi-step flow: name → target role → interview types → resume upload (optional) - Resume parsing via PDF.js (client-side text extraction) - AI resume analysis via Groq LLM (skills, achievements, career trajectory) - Interview date and target company fields - Learning style preference (direct / encouraging) - Preferred session length (10 / 15 / 25 minutes) #### Dashboard (`/dashboard`) - **Home** — Session history with score trends, readiness score, streak tracking, AI insights, smart scheduling suggestions, upcoming goals, prep plan, week activity heatmap, notifications - **Sessions** (`/dashboard/sessions`) — Filterable/searchable session list with type, score, date, duration. **Authentication:** Supabase JWT-based auth on all API endpoints. Auth bypass only in local dev when Supabase unconfigured. - **CORS:** Origin allowlist with Vercel preview wildcard. All endpoints validate origin. - **CSRF:** Origin header validation on all state-changing POST endpoints (evaluate, generate-questions, create-order, verify-payment, cancel-subscription). - **Rate Limiting:** Per-IP rate limiting via Upstash Redis (with in-memory fallback). Separate buckets per endpoint. - **IP Security:** Uses Vercel's `x-real-ip` header (not spoofable) with `x-forwarded-for` fallback. - **Payment Security:** HMAC-SHA256 signature verification, server-side amount validation against Razorpay API, duplicate payment protection (profile + payments table checks), subscription tier checks. - **Prompt Injection:** Multi-layer sanitization — strips role markers, LLM tokens (`<|im_start|>`), code blocks, JSON role injection, "ignore previous instructions" patterns, control characters. UX Gap Analysis: 12 Critical Gaps in the Market ### Gap 1: No Luxury or Premium Positioning **The problem:** Every competitor looks like generic SaaS — blue/white color schemes, stock illustrations, cluttered dashboards. None conveys the sense of quality that senior professionals expect from a $29–$199 product. Marcus (our executive persona) will close the tab in 3 seconds if the landing page looks like it was designed for interns. **Who suffers:** Senior professionals who equate design quality with product quality. Coaches who won't recommend cheap-looking tools to $8K clients. **Competitors affected:** All of them. Final Round AI, Exponent, and Huru look interchangeable. Yoodli looks like an enterprise tool. Interviewing.io is functional but unremarkable. **Level Up recommendation:** - Invest heavily in the landing page experience — cinematic typography, parallax, 3D/WebGL hero - Maintain the dark, sophisticated palette throughout - This is the single biggest differentiator in first impressions — no competitor is even attempting this --- ### Gap 2: No Real-Time Conversational AI Interviews **The problem:** Most AI tools use a batch format — show a question, record an answer, show the next question. This doesn't simulate real interview pressure. The AI doesn't listen, doesn't adapt, doesn't follow up. It's a glorified flashcard app. **Who suffers:** All users, but especially senior professionals who face unscripted, conversational executive interviews (not predictable behavioral rounds). **Competitors affected:** Final Round AI, Huru, Exponent (AI grading is post-session), Google Interview Warmup. Only Interviewing.io offers real-time conversation, but with humans at $179+/session. **Level Up recommendation:** - Real-time conversational AI (Anthropic Claude) is the core product differentiator - AI asks → user responds → AI processes → AI follows up dynamically - This bridges the gap between $179 human interviewers and $0 question banks - Market this heavily: "The only AI that actually interviews you" --- ### Gap 3: Generic, Non-Personalized Questions **The problem:** Most tools pull from static question banks. They don't read your resume. They don't know you're interviewing for a VP of Sales role at a SaaS company vs. a Product Manager at a fintech startup. The questions feel random and irrelevant. **Who suffers:** All users, but especially senior professionals with niche, role-specific interview experiences. **Competitors affected:** Huru (20K questions but static library), Google Interview Warmup (only 5 per industry), Exponent (peer-generated, not AI-tailored), Final Round AI (curated but not personalized). **Level Up recommendation:** - Resume + job context input is required before every session - AI generates questions from the user's specific background and target role - This is table stakes for senior professionals — generic questions = immediate churn - Highlight this in marketing: "Questions tailored to YOUR resume, YOUR target role" --- ### Gap 4: Surface-Level, Generic Feedback **The problem:** "Good answer" or "Try to be more specific" isn't helpful to a VP who needs to know their answer undersold their team's revenue growth by 40%. Most tools provide pass/fail verdicts or vague encouragement, not actionable coaching. **Who suffers:** All users, but especially experienced professionals who already know the basics. **Competitors affected:** Huru (generic feedback noted in reviews), Final Round AI ("surface-level for experienced professionals"), Google Interview Warmup (self-assessment only), Interview Sidekick (no feedback at all). **Level Up recommendation:** - AI feedback must be specific and role-contextualized - Score categories (communication, technical depth, leadership, strategic thinking) with numeric scores AND qualitative explanations - Expandable suggestion cards with concrete improvement actions: "You mentioned managing a $2M budget — consider quantifying the efficiency gains to strengthen this answer" - This is what converts free users to paid: feedback quality that surprises and teaches --- ### Gap 5: No B2B Coach/Firm Management **The problem:** No major AI interview tool serves the B2B coaching market. Coaches currently use spreadsheets to track client progress, manually schedule practice sessions, and have no data to demonstrate ROI. Outplacement firms (INTOO, LHH) have platforms but they're legacy, expensive, and don't include AI practice. **Who suffers:** Career coaches (Dana persona), outplacement firms (James persona), and their coached clients (Aaliyah persona). **Competitors affected:** All AI tools lack B2B features entirely. Yoodli has a basic team tier but no invite system, client management, or trial pilot. Enterprise outplacement firms have dashboards but no AI-driven practice. **Level Up recommendation:** - This is Level Up's strongest strategic advantage — nobody else is building this - Admin dashboard, client invite system, per-client progress tracking, bulk onboarding - Per-seat pricing that scales with coach's business - 1-month free pilot → annual license conversion creates a natural sales funnel - Target coaching communities and outplacement firms directly --- ### Gap 6: No Progress Tracking or Improvement Analytics **The problem:** Sessions feel isolated. Users complete a mock, get feedback, and start fresh next time with no memory. There's no improvement curve, no trend analysis, no "you've improved 23% in communication over 5 sessions." **Who suffers:** Priya (tracks scores in personal spreadsheets because no tool does it), coaches who want to show client improvement data, firms who need to report ROI. **Competitors affected:** Final Round AI (no tracking), Interviewing.io (no tracking across sessions), Huru (no progress tracking noted), Exponent (no cross-session analytics). **Level Up recommendation:** - Dashboard tracks improvement over time with session history - Pro tier unlocks deeper analytics: score trends, category improvement, session comparison - This alone justifies Pro pricing for data-driven users like Priya - B2B admin gets aggregate analytics across all clients --- ### Gap 7: Intimidating Onboarding and High Friction **The problem:** Tools either require credit cards upfront (Final Round AI), demand video recording (Huru, Exponent), or have confusing pricing pages with 4+ tiers. New users bounce before experiencing any value. **Who suffers:** Privacy-conscious executives (Marcus), skeptical coached clients (Aaliyah), anyone evaluating tools quickly. **Competitors affected:** Final Round AI (credit card required, confusing pricing), Interviewing.io ($179 minimum commitment), Huru (video recording is default), Exponent (peer matching adds scheduling friction). **Level Up recommendation:** - 1 free mock interview, no credit card, no video (audio-only) - Multi-step signup with auto-save: don't ask for everything upfront - Prove value in the first session — feedback quality is the conversion hook - Upgrade prompt appears only AFTER the user has experienced the product - Aaliyah (coached client) needs: invite link → 3 clicks → first interview --- ### Gap 8: Poor Mobile Experience **The problem:** Most competitors were built desktop-first and treat mobile as an afterthought. Responsive layouts break, interview players don't work on mobile browsers, and admin dashboards are unusable on phones. **Who suffers:** Aaliyah (practices between appointments on her phone), Priya (uses iPad after hours), any user who doesn't have a dedicated desktop session. **Competitors affected:** Interviewing.io (desktop-only sessions), Exponent (partial mobile), Final Round AI (mobile is secondary). **Level Up recommendation:** - All flows must work at 375px — this is a hard requirement, not a nice-to-have - Sidebar → hamburger menu on mobile - Audio recording must work in mobile Safari and Chrome - Feedback review should be pleasant to read on a phone --- ### Gap 9: No Data Privacy Controls **The problem:** Most tools don't let users export or delete their data. Privacy policies are buried. Retention periods are undefined. For privacy-conscious professionals discussing career moves (Marcus), and coaches managing client data (Dana, James), this is a dealbreaker. **Who suffers:** GDPR-aware users, executives discussing sensitive career moves, coaches with legal obligations around client data. **Competitors affected:** Most competitors have no visible export/delete functionality. Interview Sidekick is the exception (explicit data deletion). No tool offers configurable retention policies. **Level Up recommendation:** - GDPR compliance as a feature, not a checkbox - Export data in user's choice of format (JSON, CSV, PDF) - Delete account with clear retention period selection (30/90/180 days) - B2B admin: configurable data retention policy per organization - Surface privacy controls prominently in account settings — don't bury them --- ### Gap 10: No Accessibility Standards **The problem:** WCAG compliance is virtually absent from the competitive landscape. Interview players aren't keyboard navigable, screen reader support is nonexistent, color contrast fails on dark UIs, and audio-only users have no text alternatives. **Who suffers:** Users with disabilities, users in accessibility-mandated environments (government, large enterprises), B2B firms required to use compliant tools. **Competitors affected:** No competitor prominently advertises WCAG compliance. Testing suggests most would fail basic audits. **Level Up recommendation:** - WCAG 2.1 AA is a first-class requirement, not a post-launch audit - Keyboard navigation on all interactive elements - Screen reader support with ARIA labels - TTS + text display toggle gives users a choice in how questions are delivered - `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on landing page animations - This is both ethical and a B2B sales advantage: firms can't use non-compliant tools --- ### Gap 11: Content Stagnation and Question Fatigue **The problem:** Tools with static question banks get stale fast. Users report seeing the same questions after 3–4 sessions. AI-generated questions that don't evolve with the user's responses create a "groundhog day" effect. **Who suffers:** Power users who practice frequently (Marcus at 4–5x/week, Priya at 2–3x/week). **Competitors affected:** Huru (static 20K library), Google Interview Warmup (5 questions per field), Exponent (repeated questions in peer matching). **Level Up recommendation:** - AI generates fresh questions every session from resume + job context - Real-time follow-ups mean no two sessions are the same - Practice challenges (Pro tier) add structured variety: "C-level mock", "Behavioral round", "Technical deep-dive" - Session memory: AI can reference previous session feedback to focus on weak areas --- ### Gap 12: No Emotional Design or Encouragement **The problem:** Interview prep is emotionally loaded — users are often anxious, recently laid off, or under career pressure. Most tools are clinical and transactional. Feedback feels like a grade, not coaching. There's no encouragement, no celebration of improvement, no acknowledgment of the vulnerability involved. **Who suffers:** Aaliyah (recently laid off, skeptical, needs encouragement), Marcus (embarrassed to need practice). **Competitors affected:** All competitors treat interview prep as a functional task, not an emotional journey. **Level Up recommendation:** - Feedback tone should be constructive and encouraging, not judgmental - Celebrate improvement: "Your communication score improved 18% this week" - Acknowledge difficulty: "Executive interviews are uniquely challenging — here's how you're progressing" - First-session experience must validate the user's decision to try the product - Design loading states and transitions that feel calm and confident, not anxious --- ## Competitive Pricing Comparison | Tool | Free Tier | Entry Price | Premium Price | B2B | | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | | **Level Up** | 1 free mock (no card) | $69/month | $199/quarter (Pro) | Per-seat annual | | Final Round AI | No | $25/month (annual) | $299.99/month | No | | Yoodli | Yes (limited) | $8/month | $25/user/month (team) | Enterprise custom | | Interviewing.io | No | $179/session | $2,000/3 sessions | No | | Exponent | 5 mocks/month | $79/month | — | No | | Huru | Yes (1 report) | $24.99/month | $99/year | No | | Interview Sidekick | Yes | $10/month | — | No | | Google Warmup | Free (shutting down) | — | — | No | | Pelgo | Unknown | Unknown | Enterprise | Enterprise | | INTOO/LHH | No | Enterprise contract | $500–$5K/employee | Enterprise only | **Level Up's pricing position:** Premium but accessible.
